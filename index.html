About

I'm a computer scientist primarily interested in abstract cryptography, machine learning, hardware conscious software development, and meaty involved projects. I hold a first class MEng Computer Science degree from The University of Bristol.

When I'm not programming I tend to be spending time with my band, The Pylons, with whom I've played guitar for over 6 years.

Evolved Binary Arithmetic Circuits and Dynamic Problems
MEng Dissertation

During the final year of my degree I explored using genetic algorithms to design simple binary addition/subtraction circuits on a simulated FPGA, attempting to discern the value of such a scheme to act as a robust fault recovery mechanism.

Bespoke Superscalar Processor Simulator

Designed and simulated a superscalar processor complete with a 5 stage 4-way superscalar execution pipeline, a unified reservation station, reorder buffer, out of order execution, register renaming, and configurable static and dynamic branch prediction.

LockDown
An Asymmetric VR Multiplayer Game

Developed as part of an agile team on the Unity game engine, LockDown, pits a wily infiltrator (in a VR headset) against a team of security "professionals". The infiltrator is attempting to evade the security team and steal as much corporate loot as possible, all the while searching for their ultimate prize. Upon "recovering" the final item the infiltrator has to escape with his life (and booty) intact.

TaddyPole
An Educational Game About Tadpoles

This game was developed in the Unity engine in conjunction with The University of Bristol's Life Sciences department as part of the public outreach associated with a research project. It is designed to teach young children about the lifecycle of tadpoles and currently has over 200,000 downloads across iOS, Android, and Windows 10.
